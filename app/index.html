How to Count in Italian

Counting in Italian is easy as long as you know how to count from 1 to 19, by tens up to 100, and account for a few exceptions.

The Basic Rules

For the numbers 20 through 99, take the number in the tens place (venti,trenta, quaranta, etc) and affix one of the numbers uno through nove.

If the number ends in 1 or 8, drop the final vowel from the tens number.

If the number ends in 3, tre becomes tré.

21 ventuno 22 ventidue
23 ventitré 24 ventiquattro
55 cinquantacinque 66 sessantasei
88 ottantotto 89 ottantanove

Counting 100 - 999

For 100, 200, 300, 400, you add 2, 3, 4, and so forth to cento. (You do not add anything before cento if the number is 100.) Thus, 200 is duecento, 300 is trecento, and 400 is quattrocento. You add the number 1 through 99 to the hundreds value to get the other combinations of numbers:

147 centoquarantasette
888 ottocentottantotto

IMPORTANT! When cento is followed by otto or ottanta, you drop the o from cento.

Counting 1000 - 9999

For numbers above 999, you will use mille for 1000 and mila for 2000 (duemila), 3000 (tremila), 4000(quattromila), etc.

Numbers are chained together like this:

1048 millequarantotto

8888 ottomilaottocentottantotto

Creating Accented Characters

Mac:
The easiest way to make the accent in tré is to enable the Extended US Keyboard. Hold down the e key and select 2 from the menu that pops up.

Windows:
Use the Windows ALT Code (ALT+0233).
